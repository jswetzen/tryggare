# Personal Data Breach Notification Process — {{ORGANISATION_NAME}}

*Template — adapt roles and contacts before relying on it. Last updated: {{DATE}}.*

A personal data breach is any security incident leading to accidental or
unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure of, or access to
personal data — including children's data held in this system.

## 1. Detect & contain (immediately)

- Anyone who suspects a breach reports it to {{BREACH_LEAD}} without delay.
- {{BREACH_LEAD}} takes immediate steps to contain it (e.g. revoke a
  compromised login, take the system offline, rotate credentials).
- Preserve evidence and the audit log; do not delete data that may be needed to
  investigate.

## 2. Assess (within 24 hours)

Determine:

- What data and how many individuals are affected (note if children / health
  data are involved — this raises the risk).
- The likely consequences and the risk to individuals' rights and freedoms.

## 3. Notify the supervisory authority (within 72 hours)

- If the breach is likely to result in a risk to individuals, notify
  {{SUPERVISORY_AUTHORITY}} within **72 hours** of becoming aware.
- If notification is later than 72 hours, include reasons for the delay.
- Include: nature of the breach, categories/approximate numbers affected, likely
  consequences, and measures taken.

## 4. Notify affected individuals (without undue delay)

- If the breach is likely to result in a **high** risk to individuals (likely
  for children's health data), inform the affected parents/guardians directly,
  in clear language, with advice on protective steps and a contact point.

## 5. Record & review

- Log every breach (even those not notified) with facts, effects and remedial
  action — this is required for accountability.
- After resolution, review what happened and update controls to prevent
  recurrence.
